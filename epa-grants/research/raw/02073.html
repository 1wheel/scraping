Stabilization of Eroding Shorelines in Estuarine Wave Eliminates with Constructed Fringe Wetlands Incorporating Offshore Breakwaters

EPA Grant Number: R827072C003
Subproject: this is subproject number 003 , established and managed by the Center Director under grant R827072
(EPA does not fund or establish subprojects; EPA awards and manages the overall grant for this center).
Center: Alabama Center For Estuarine Studies (ACES)
Center Director: Shipp, Robert L.
Title: Stabilization of Eroding Shorelines in Estuarine Wave Eliminates with Constructed Fringe Wetlands Incorporating Offshore Breakwaters
Investigators: Douglass, Scott L. , Stout, Judy P.
Institution: University of South Alabama
EPA Project Officer: Sergeant, Anne
Project Period: October 1, 1999 through September 30, 2000
RFA: Alabama Center For Estuarine Studies (ACES) (1999) RFA Text |  Recipients Lists
Research Category: Ecological Restoration , Targeted Research

Objective:

This research is investigating the use of low-crested offshore breakwaters in the construction of fringe wetlands. Although some engineering guidance exists for the functional design of offshore breakwaters for beach creation, guidance for their use for wetlands stabilization is very limited. Results of this research to date focus on evaluating the equilibrium shape of shorelines adjacent to offshore breakwaters and characterizing the wave climate at a bay site with standard wind-wave models.

Expected Results:

Equilibrium shoreline shapes are being investigated to minimize the extent of breakwaters required. It is envisioned that wetlands could be created behind a stabilized low elevation sand bar much as natural fringe wetlands develop a "wrack line" on their bayward side in energetic environments. These could be built with sand and stabilized much as beaches are stabilized by offshore, segmented breakwaters.
